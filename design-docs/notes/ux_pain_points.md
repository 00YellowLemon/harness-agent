# Identifying and Categorizing UX Pain Points

## Overview
Hurdles or problems in the customer's experience are known as **pain points**. Identifying these pain points is the first step toward designing exceptional experiences that address user needs.

## Characteristics of Pain Points
Pain points are diverse. They can be:
- Broad or specific
- Severe or generally insignificant
- Extremely obvious or hidden
- All pain points incur a cost to the user (e.g., wasted time, extra steps, or financial loss).

## The Three Categories of Pain Points
Pain points fall into three distinct levels, mirroring the three levels of customer experience.

### 1. Interaction Level Pain Points
- **Definition:** Problems occurring during a specific, isolated interaction.
- **Detection Method:** Can be detected through targeted user research such as usability tests. Traditionally, UX has focused heavily on this level.
- **Example:** Calling customer support and being passed from person to person. The user must repeatedly explain their issue to multiple departments, often receiving conflicting information.
- **Impact:** Causes wasted time, confusion, and frustration.

### 2. Journey Level Pain Points
- **Definition:** Problems that span the entirety of a user's process to achieve a specific goal.
- **Detection Method:** Found through a combination of exploratory research, such as:
  - User interviews
  - Diary studies
  - Field studies
  - Customer journey mapping
  This allows researchers to collect various data points across the entire journey and assess how interactions come together.
- **Example:** A user places an order but doesn't receive it for months. The long duration between purchase and completion, coupled with a lack of upfront communication about wait times and the need to call support for updates, makes the entire journey a pain point.
- **Impact:** Creates frustration across a timeline, rather than just at a single touchpoint.

### 3. Relationship Level Pain Points
- **Definition:** Problems uncovered over long periods of time that affect a person's lifetime experience with an organization.
- **Detection Method:** Identified via:
  - Benchmarking surveys (brand loyalty, likelihood to recommend, overall customer satisfaction)
  - Analytics data
  - Evaluating technical infrastructure and data management
- **Example:** Paying for a premium streaming service subscription but still being forced to watch constant ads. This deviates from industry norms and betrays the user's expectations.
- **Impact:** Diminishes brand trust, loyalty, and overall relationship quality.

## Prioritization and Strategy
- While pain points are never ideal, it is **not cost-efficient** to try to solve all of them simultaneously.
- Identifying the category a pain point belongs to helps teams weigh its impact.
- Trade-offs must be made to strategically apply UX resources.
- **Process:** Uncover pain points -> Categorize and prioritize them -> Explore potential solutions -> Plot the work onto a future roadmap.
