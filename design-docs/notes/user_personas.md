# Notes on User Personas

## What is a User Persona?
* A persona is a single, personified representation of a subset of a target audience.
* This subset shares similar behaviors, goals, motivations, and needs related to a specific product or service.
* Creating personas requires conducting research with real people representing the target audience.
* The collected research is analyzed to identify segments or subgroups with common characteristics.

## Why are Personas Useful?

### 1. They Create a Shared Understanding
* Team members often have differing understandings of what users need.
* Personas replace fragmented assumptions with specific, agreed-upon representations.
* Organizing customer knowledge through research helps validate or invalidate team members' assumptions.
* This shared understanding leads to confident design decisions on behalf of identified user segments.

### 2. They Help Remember and Apply Knowledge About Users
* User research data exists in many formats (analytics, surveys), making it difficult to grasp cumulative insights.
* Personas consolidate and humanize complex data.
* Humanizing data makes it easier to consume, empathize with, and remember (especially when presented as stories).
* Rich customer understanding becomes ingrained, ensuring that every micro-decision during design and development is data-informed.

## Key Takeaway
Personas drive user-centered decisions by creating alignment within the team and ensuring user data is memorable and continuously applied throughout the design and development process.
