# User Story Mapping vs. Customer Journey Mapping

This document provides a breakdown of the differences between User Story Mapping and Customer Journey Mapping based on UX conference discussions, focusing on perspective and purpose.

## Customer Journey Map (CJM)

*   **Perspective:** The User (Person)
*   **Purpose:** Share understanding of people's needs and the processes they go through when interacting with a product or service.
*   **Source/Input:** Often an output of discovery research.
*   **What it shows:**
    *   Steps the user goes through to complete a goal.
    *   User's thoughts and feelings.
    *   Channels and devices the user interacts with along the way.
*   **Key Outcomes:**
    *   Surfaces user pain points.
    *   Identifies opportunities to improve the journey.
    *   Highlights problematic touchpoints.

## User Story Map (USM)

*   **Perspective:** The Product
*   **Purpose:** Detail steps taken within the product itself to complete an activity (e.g., checking an account balance, where logging in is the first step).
*   **Source/Input:** Can act as a high-level input for epics, user stories, and tasks that feed the product backlog, replacing lengthy requirements documents.
*   **Key Outcomes:**
    *   Helps teams discuss features and requirements.
    *   Assists in working through design details and planning implementation timelines.
    *   Connects issues uncovered in Customer Journey Maps to concrete solutions within the product to resolve those issues.

## Combining and Evolving Maps

While effective separately, these two mapping methods can work well together:
*   **CJM to USM:** A Customer Journey Map can evolve into a User Story Map by adding product-focused activities, steps, and details that users interact with to complete their tasks.
*   **USM to CJM:** A User Story Map can evolve into a Customer Journey Map by adding the user's thoughts, feelings, channels, and devices.

## Summary

*   **Customer Journey Map:** Focuses on the *person's* perspective.
*   **User Story Map:** Focuses on the *product's* perspective.
*   **Both** are essential tools for planning and executing meaningful product iterations.
